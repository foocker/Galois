## Design Context

### Users
Galois serves mathematically serious users: researchers, advanced students, theorem provers, and technical writers who bring Markdown-formatted mathematical questions, notes, proof attempts, or paper excerpts into a focused research environment. Their job is not casual chat; they want a durable workspace that can explain concepts, answer difficult problems, explore research-level directions, search related theorems, assist paper reading, and polish mathematical writing.

### Brand Personality
The product should feel calm, rigorous, and scholarly. Galois should evoke confidence, precision, and concentration rather than entertainment or generic AI chat. The interface should respect long-form mathematical thinking: dense enough for real work, quiet enough for sustained attention, and transparent about research progress and verification state.

### Aesthetic Direction
The first UI should be an academic-calm research workbench. Prefer a refined light-first interface with warm paper neutrals, ink-like typography, restrained mathematical ornament, and clear workspace structure. Avoid neon AI dashboards, glassmorphism, purple-blue gradients, chat-app clichés, and decorative “AI magic” visuals. Future platform capabilities should be visible as a roadmap/navigation system, while the initial MVP should enable the real flow: submit a Markdown problem, watch Galois research it, and read the resulting answer.

### Design Principles
- Treat math work as a research session, not a chat thread.
- Make system progress legible: queued, reasoning, verification, repair, complete, failed.
- Keep the primary writing/reading surface calm, typographic, and formula-friendly.
- Show future capabilities explicitly without pretending they are all available now.
- Favor durable artifacts, run history, and citations/proof states over transient conversation UI.
