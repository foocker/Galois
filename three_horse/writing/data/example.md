## Working Title

Compactness and extrema for continuous real-valued maps

## Research Problem

Let $X$ be a compact topological space and let $f:X \to \mathbb{R}$ be continuous. Explain why $f$ is bounded and attains its maximum and minimum.

## Draft

We prove that compactness implies the function has maximum and minimum. Since the image of compact is compact and compact subsets of real line are closed and bounded, the result is clear.

Improve this into a short mathematical paper-style exposition. Add a clear title, abstract, proof prose, citation notes, and a self-review report.
