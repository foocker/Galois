Problem.

Characterize the algebraic varieties that fail to admit power-saving Elekes-Szab\'o-type incidence bounds. In the exceptional case, describe the underlying commutative algebraic-group structure and the extra endomorphism structure responsible for the obstruction.

Context.

Bays and Breuillard generalize the Elekes-Szab\'o theorem to arbitrary arity and dimension and characterize the complex algebraic varieties without power saving. Their approach uses Hrushovski's framework of pseudo-finite dimensions and the abelian group configuration theorem, and it also yields sum-product consequences for elliptic curves.

Benchmark formulation.

Given a variety with unusually large intersections with Cartesian products of finite sets, decide whether it belongs to the exceptional algebraic-group class or else prove a power-saving bound.

References.

M. Bays and E. Breuillard, Projective geometries arising from Elekes-Szab\'o problems, Ann. Sci. \'Ec. Norm. Sup\'er. 54 (2021), 627-681.
