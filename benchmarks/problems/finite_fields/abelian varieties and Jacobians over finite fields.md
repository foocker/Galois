Problem.

Fix a prime power $q$ and an integer $g \ge 1$. Characterize the $g$-dimensional abelian varieties over $\mathbb{F}_q$ that are $\mathbb{F}_q$-isogenous to Jacobians of smooth projective curves of genus $g$. Equivalently, which isogeny classes contain a Jacobian?

Context.

Katz's expository paper collects open questions about curves and abelian varieties over finite fields and the relations between them. A central theme is the gap between arbitrary abelian varieties and those that arise from curves via the Jacobian construction.

Benchmark formulation.

Given an explicit isogeny class over $\mathbb{F}_q$, decide whether it contains a Jacobian, or produce structural obstructions or positive criteria.

References.

N. M. Katz, Some Open Questions about Curves and Abelian Varieties over Finite Fields, Vietnam J. Math. 51 (2023), 715-720.
