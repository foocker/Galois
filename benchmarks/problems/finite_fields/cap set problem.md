Problem.

For $n \ge 1$, determine the largest possible size of a subset $A \subseteq \mathbb{F}_3^n$ that contains no nontrivial three-term arithmetic progression, i.e. no distinct $x,y,z \in A$ with
$$
x+z=2y.
$$

Context.

This is the cap set problem, one of the central finite-field models in arithmetic combinatorics. Modern surveys discuss it as a model case for Roth- and Szemeredi-type questions over finite fields, and the polynomial method gives the best known upper bounds.

Benchmark formulation.

Given $n$, construct large progression-free subsets of $\mathbb{F}_3^n$, or prove upper bounds for their maximum size.

References.

S. Peluse, Finite field models in arithmetic combinatorics -- twenty years on, in Surveys in Combinatorics 2024, pp. 159-200.

E. Croot, V. Lev, and P. Pach, Progression-free sets in $\mathbb{Z}_4^n$ are exponentially small, Ann. of Math. 185 (2017), 331-337.

J. Ellenberg and D. Gijswijt, On large subsets of $\mathbb{F}_q^n$ with no three-term arithmetic progression, Ann. of Math. 185 (2017), 339-343.
