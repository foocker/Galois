Problem.

Let $q$ be a prime power and let $r \ge 2$. Let $A_1,\dots,A_r$ be $\mathbb{F}_q$-affine hyperplanes in $\mathbb{F}_{q^r}$ that are in general position, and define
$$
S_{\mathcal C}^*=\mathbb{F}_{q^r}^* \setminus \bigcup_{i=1}^r A_i.
$$
Does $S_{\mathcal C}^*$ contain a primitive element of $\mathbb{F}_{q^r}$?

Context.

This is the affine-hyperplane avoidance problem for primitive elements. Fernandes and Reis studied it for $q \ge 7$, and Bagger and Punch developed a modified prime sieve that applies to primitive elements in general subsets of $\mathbb{F}_{q^r}$ and used it to improve the known results in this setting.

Benchmark formulation.

Given $q$, $r$, and explicit affine hyperplanes in general position, either produce a primitive element in $S_{\mathcal C}^*$ or prove that one must exist under explicit hypotheses on $(q,r)$.

References.

A. Fernandes and L. Reis, On primitive elements of finite fields avoiding affine hyperplanes, Finite Fields Appl. 76 (2021), 101911.

G. K. Bagger and J. Punch, The modified prime sieve for primitive elements in finite fields, arXiv:2507.21515.
