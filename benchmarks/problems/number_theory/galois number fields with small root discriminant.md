Problem.

Let
$$
\Omega = 8 \pi e^\gamma \approx 44.7632.
$$
For a finite group $G$, identify the set
$$
\mathcal{K}(G,\Omega)
$$
of Galois number fields $K/\mathbb{Q}$ with
$$
\operatorname{Gal}(K/\mathbb{Q}) \cong G
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
\operatorname{rd}(K) < \Omega,
$$
where $\operatorname{rd}(K)$ denotes the root discriminant of $K$.

Context.

Jones and Roberts posed this problem explicitly and carried it out for several groups, including $A_4$, $A_5$, $A_6$, $S_4$, $S_5$, and $S_6$. They also conjectured that for all but finitely many groups $G$, the set $\mathcal{K}(G,\Omega)$ is empty.

Benchmark formulation.

For a specified transitive group $G$, either classify all fields in $\mathcal{K}(G,\Omega)$, prove that the set is empty, or construct new examples with root discriminant below $\Omega$.

References.

J. W. Jones and D. P. Roberts, Galois number fields with small root discriminant, J. Number Theory 122 (2007), 379-407.

J. W. Jones and D. P. Roberts, A database of number fields, LMS J. Comput. Math. 17 (2014), 595-618.
