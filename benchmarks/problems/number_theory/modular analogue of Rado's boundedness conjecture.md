Problem.

Let $p$ be a prime, and let $a_1,\dots,a_k \in \mathbb{Z}$ have the property that
$$
\sum_{i \in I} a_i \not\equiv 0 \pmod p
$$
for every nonempty subset $I \subseteq \{1,\dots,k\}$. Does there exist a function $f(k)$ such that $(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^\times$ admits an $f(k)$-coloring with no monochromatic solution to
$$
a_1x_1 + \cdots + a_kx_k = 0?
$$

Context.

Fox and Kleitman proposed this as a modular analogue of Rado's boundedness conjecture, and Green highlighted it as a natural open problem in arithmetic Ramsey theory. It appears to be open even for $k=3$, and Green explicitly notes that the answer may be negative.

Benchmark formulation.

For fixed small $k$ and explicit coefficient patterns $(a_1,\dots,a_k)$, construct colorings of $(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^\times$ avoiding monochromatic solutions, or prove lower and upper bounds for the number of colors required.

References.

J. Fox and D. Kleitman, On Rado's boundedness conjecture, J. Combin. Theory Ser. A 113 (2006), 84-100.

B. Green, 100 Open Problems, December 2025 update. See Problem 21 and the accompanying comments.
