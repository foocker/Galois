Problem.

Fix an elliptic curve $E/\mathbb{Q}$. Among the quadratic twists $E^d$, prove that 50% have rank $0$ and 50% have rank $1$, with density $0$ for twists of rank at least $2$.

Context.

This is Goldfeld's conjecture for the quadratic twist family of $E$. Smith proved major Selmer-group analogues: in earlier work he established the expected $2^\infty$-Selmer distribution in important special cases, and in 2025 he proved that for every elliptic curve $E/\mathbb{Q}$, 50% of the quadratic twists have $2^\infty$-Selmer corank $0$ and 50% have $2^\infty$-Selmer corank $1$. As a consequence, the Birch-Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture implies Goldfeld's conjecture.

Benchmark formulation.

Given an explicit elliptic curve $E/\mathbb{Q}$, study the distribution of $2$-Selmer or $2^\infty$-Selmer ranks in its quadratic twist family, and connect these statistics to the rank distribution predicted by Goldfeld.

References.

A. Smith, $2^\infty$-Selmer groups, $2^\infty$-class groups, and Goldfeld's conjecture, Ann. of Math. 194 (2021), 277-364.

A. Smith, The Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture implies Goldfeld's conjecture, arXiv:2503.17619, submitted March 22, 2025.
