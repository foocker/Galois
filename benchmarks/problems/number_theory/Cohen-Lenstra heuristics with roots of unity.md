Problem.

Let $p$ be an odd prime. Determine the distribution of the $p$-primary parts of class groups in families of number fields whose base field, or relevant intermediate fields, contain $p$-th roots of unity. What replaces the classical Cohen-Lenstra-Martinet weight
$$
\frac{1}{|\operatorname{Aut}(G)|}
$$
for a finite abelian $p$-group $G$?

Context.

The classical Cohen-Lenstra-Martinet heuristics predict class-group distributions by weighting groups essentially by the reciprocal of their automorphism counts. Malle showed that this prediction can fail systematically in the presence of roots of unity and proposed modified distributions that fit the available data much better.

Benchmark formulation.

Given a concrete family of number fields with roots of unity present, compute the $p$-parts of class groups, compare them with the classical heuristic, and formulate or justify the corrected distribution.

References.

G. Malle, Cohen-Lenstra heuristic and roots of unity, J. Number Theory 128 (2008), 2823-2835.

J. S. Ellenberg, A. Venkatesh, and C. Westerland, Homological stability for Hurwitz spaces and the Cohen-Lenstra conjecture over function fields, Ann. of Math. 183 (2016), 729-786.
