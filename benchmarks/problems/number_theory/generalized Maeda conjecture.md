Problem.

Fix a positive integer $N$. Determine the number of Galois orbits of newforms in
$$
S_k^{\mathrm{new}}(\Gamma_0(N))
$$
as the even weight $k$ varies. Is the number of non-CM Galois orbits eventually constant as $k \to \infty$, depending only on $N$?

Context.

For level $N=1$, this is Maeda's conjecture. For higher level, Tsaknias proposed that the number of non-CM orbits should eventually stabilize. Dieulefait, Pacetti, and Tsaknias gave lower bounds coming from local invariants and asked whether these bounds are eventually sharp, which would amount to a higher-level analogue of Maeda's conjecture.

Benchmark formulation.

Given a fixed level $N$, compute Galois orbit counts for many weights, compare them with the lower bounds coming from local data such as Atkin-Lehner signs and inertial types, and prove stabilization or equality in specific families.

References.

P. Tsaknias, A possible generalization of Maeda's conjecture, in Computations with Modular Forms 2011, Springer, 2014, pp. 317-329.

L. V. Dieulefait, A. Pacetti, and P. Tsaknias, On the number of Galois orbits of newforms, J. Eur. Math. Soc. 23 (2021), 2833-2860.
