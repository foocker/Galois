Problem.

Does there exist a constant $c>1$ such that every noncyclotomic polynomial
$$
P(x) \in \mathbb{Z}[x]
$$
satisfies
$$
M(P) \ge c,
$$
where $M(P)$ is the Mahler measure of $P$?

Context.

This is Lehmer's problem. The smallest known Mahler measure greater than $1$ is achieved by Lehmer's degree-10 polynomial, with value approximately
$$
1.176280818\ldots
$$
Searches for smaller examples naturally focus on reciprocal polynomials and Salem numbers, but no general lower bound greater than $1$ is known.

Benchmark formulation.

Search for low-Mahler-measure polynomials in structured families such as reciprocal polynomials, Salem polynomials, or bounded-height integer polynomials, or prove explicit lower bounds within such families.

References.

D. W. Boyd, Speculations Concerning the Range of Mahler's Measure, Canad. Math. Bull. 24 (1981), 453-469.

F. Brunault and W. Zudilin, Many Variations of Mahler Measures, Cambridge University Press, 2020. See Chapter 2, "Lehmer's problem".
